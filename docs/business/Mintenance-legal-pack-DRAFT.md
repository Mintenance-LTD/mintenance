# DOCUMENT 3, Mintenance Vetting Standard

_This is both an internal checklist and the basis for what you can honestly claim publicly. The rule
is simple: only claim what you actually do, and do it consistently for every Contractor before they
take paid work._

## Why this exists

Your entire pitch is trust. If you say Contractors are "vetted", you must be able to show what that
means and that you did it. This protects users and protects you from a mis-selling or negligence
claim. It is a launch-blocker for taking real money.

## Minimum checks before a Contractor can take a paid job

1. **Identity:** verified government ID; name matches account and business.
2. **Right to work / trade in the UK:** confirmed.
3. **Public liability insurance:** valid certificate on file, minimum £[AMOUNT] cover, with expiry
   tracked.
4. **Trade-specific registration and qualifications (as applicable):**
   - Gas work: **Gas Safe registered** (verify the registration number on the Gas Safe Register).
   - Electrical work: registered with a competent-person scheme (for example NICEIC, NAPIT) where
     relevant.
   - Other trades: relevant certifications/qualifications evidenced.
5. **Business details:** trading name, contact, and (if a company) company number.
6. **References or proof of recent work** where available.

## Ongoing

- Track insurance and registration **expiry dates**; suspend Contractors whose cover lapses.
- Act on reviews and complaints; remove Contractors who fail standards.
- Re-verify periodically (for example annually).

## What you can honestly say in marketing

- ✅ "We check ID, insurance, and trade registrations before contractors can take paid work."
- ✅ "Gas work is only carried out by Gas Safe registered engineers."
- ⚠️ Avoid absolute promises like "guaranteed" or "100% safe" unless you can back them. Prefer
  "vetted" with a clear, public explanation of what vetting includes.
- ❌ Do not claim checks you are not actually doing (for example "DBS checked" or "background
  checked") unless every Contractor genuinely has been.

## Keep evidence

For each Contractor, store the date and outcome of each check and the documents relied on. If a
dispute or claim ever arises, this record is your defence.
