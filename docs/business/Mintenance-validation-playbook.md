# Mintenance, Validation Playbook

_How to get your first 5 to 10 real, paid jobs through the platform, by people who aren't you, in
one city. For Gloire & Liam · June 2026._

> The goal of this phase is not growth. It's proof. You are answering one question: **will real
> homeowners and real contractors complete and pay for a job through Mintenance, and come back?**
> Everything here is designed to get that answer fast and cheap. Do things that don't scale. Be the
> algorithm by hand.

---

## The one metric that matters

**Completed, paid jobs through the platform by non-founders.** Target: 5 to 10 in one city over the
next 6 to 8 weeks.

Everything else (signups, followers, app installs) is a leading indicator. A completed paid job with
a happy homeowner and a paid contractor is the only thing that proves the business. A second job
from the same person is the strongest signal you can get.

---

## The riskiest assumptions you're testing

Write these on the wall and tick them off as evidence arrives:

1. **Trust:** will a homeowner pay an unknown platform instead of paying a tradesperson directly?
2. **Supply quality:** can you actually source vetted contractors who do good work, on time?
3. **Willingness to pay the fee:** will contractors accept the platform fee in exchange for the job
   and getting paid reliably?
4. **The loop:** does the full journey (post, quote, accept, pay, do the work, release, review)
   actually complete without you rescuing it?

If all four hold even a few times, you have a real business worth scaling. If one consistently
breaks, that's your most important learning, and you found it for almost nothing.

---

## Step 1: Get supply ready first (week 1)

A homeowner posting a job into an empty marketplace is the fastest way to kill trust. Line up supply
before you chase demand.

- **Recruit 3 to 5 contractors you can personally vouch for** in your chosen city. Start with trades
  you've used, trades friends rate, and local trades with good reviews you approach directly.
- **Vet them properly** using your Vetting Standard (ID, insurance, Gas Safe / registration,
  qualifications). This is also your first real test of the vetting process.
- **Be honest with them:** "We're a new local platform. I'll bring you real jobs, you get paid
  reliably and on time, and you're a founding contractor (free for life). In return I need your
  honest feedback." Tradespeople respond to "reliable work + getting paid", lead with that.
- Aim for a small spread of common trades: a plumber, an electrician, a handyperson, a decorator.
  Enough to cover the jobs most likely to come up.

**Done when:** you have at least 3 vetted contractors who've said yes and are ready to receive a job
this week.

---

## Step 2: Source the first jobs by hand (weeks 1 to 3)

Don't wait for inbound. Go find real jobs.

- **Start with warm contacts.** You and Liam almost certainly know people with a small job they've
  been putting off: a dripping tap, a dodgy socket, a fence panel, a room that needs painting. The
  first 3 to 5 jobs should come from people you can message directly.
- **The ask (copy/paste starting point):**
  > "Hey [name], we've built Mintenance, a way to get home jobs done with vetted local trades and
  > your payment protected until the work's done. I'm lining up our first few real jobs. Got
  > anything around the house that needs sorting? I'll personally match you with a vetted [city]
  > tradesperson and walk you through it. No catch, you'd be one of our founding members (free for
  > life)."
- **Then widen carefully:** local Facebook groups and Nextdoor, where people literally post "can
  anyone recommend a [trade]". Respond helpfully and offer to match them. Don't spam; be a useful
  neighbour who happens to run this.

**Done when:** you have 3 to 5 real jobs identified with a homeowner ready to go.

---

## Step 3: Concierge the job through the platform (the core test)

For each job, you are the matchmaker, but the transaction goes through the app so you can see where
it breaks.

1. Help the homeowner post the job (or watch them do it, this is gold).
2. Get one of your vetted contractors to quote.
3. Homeowner accepts and **pays through the platform** (real money, even if small). This is the
   moment that proves trust.
4. Contractor does the work. Before/after photos as the flow requires.
5. Homeowner confirms completion, funds release to the contractor.
6. Both leave a review.

**Watch the whole thing like a hawk.** Where does the homeowner hesitate? What do they ask you?
Where does the contractor get confused or drop off? Every point of friction is a finding. Take notes
during, not after.

**Two rules:**

- Don't fix the product mid-test by adding features. Note the problem, get the job done (manually if
  you must), move on.
- If something forces the payment off-platform, that's a critical finding, write it down. The
  on-platform payment is the thing you're testing.

---

## Step 4: Talk to every single user (ongoing)

Every founding member is a 20-minute conversation. This is where the real learning lives. Do it by
phone or in person, not a survey.

**Homeowner interview questions:**

- Walk me through the last time you needed a tradesperson. What happened?
- What nearly stopped you from using Mintenance? Be honest, you won't offend me.
- At the moment you paid, how did you feel? Any hesitation?
- Would you use it again? For what kind of job?
- What would you tell a friend it is, in your own words?
- (If they hesitate) what would have to be true for you to use it for a bigger job?

**Contractor interview questions:**

- How do you get work now? What's the most annoying part?
- How did getting paid through Mintenance compare to your usual way?
- Is the fee worth it for a reliable job and guaranteed payment? Where's your line?
- What nearly stopped you from taking the job?
- Would you take more jobs from us? How many a week could you handle?

**What you're listening for:** the gap between "nice idea" and "I actually needed this." Strong
signals are unprompted enthusiasm, repeat requests, and people referring others without being asked.
Weak signals are politeness and "yeah I'd probably use it sometime."

---

## Step 5: Read the results honestly (week 6 to 8)

After 5 to 10 jobs, sit down with the data and the interview notes and answer plainly:

- Did the loop complete without you rescuing it? How many times?
- Did anyone come back for a second job?
- Did contractors want more work?
- What was the single biggest reason people hesitated?

**Green light (press on):** jobs complete cleanly, at least a couple of repeat requests, contractors
asking for more. Now it's worth investing in the legal finalisation, the app store launch, and
scaling the content engine.

**Amber (fix and retest):** the loop works but one step keeps breaking (for example trust at
payment). Fix that one thing, run 5 more jobs.

**Red (rethink):** you can't get people to complete a paid job even with hand-holding. That's a hard
but cheap lesson. Talk to more users about why, and be willing to change the model. Better to learn
this now than after spending money.

## The tracking sheet (set this up today)

A single Google Sheet, two tabs. Keep it stupidly simple.

**Tab 1, Jobs**

| Job # | Date | Homeowner (name) | Postcode | Job type | Contractor | Quote £ | Paid on platform? (Y/N) | Completed? (Y/N) | Released? (Y/N) | Repeat? (Y/N) | Where it nearly broke | Notes |
| ----- | ---- | ---------------- | -------- | -------- | ---------- | ------- | ----------------------- | ---------------- | --------------- | ------------- | --------------------- | ----- |

**Tab 2, Founding members / pipeline**

| Name | Type (HO/LL/Trade) | Postcode | Source (DM, FB group, referral...) | Signed up? | Job done? | Notes / quotes from them |
| ---- | ------------------ | -------- | ---------------------------------- | ---------- | --------- | ------------------------ |

The postcode columns matter most: they tell you whether density is forming anywhere, which is the
difference between a marketplace and a list of strangers.

---

## What success in this phase looks like (recap)

- 5 to 10 completed, paid jobs through the platform, by non-founders, in one city.
- At least one or two people who came back for a second job.
- 3+ contractors who want more work.
- A clear, written answer to "what nearly stops people, and what makes them love it."
- Density starting to cluster in one area.

Hit that, and you've earned the right to spend money on scaling. Everything in the roadmap
downstream of validation assumes you got here first.
