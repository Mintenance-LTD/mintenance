## The honest reframe

You picked "brand awareness / followers" as the goal, but awareness is the _input_, not the prize.
The real target you named is **150 founding members with lifetime-free access**. Followers don't pay
rent; founding members prove the marketplace works. So this plan treats every post as a step toward
that number, and we track signups, not vanity follower counts.

Three calls I'm making for you, because with one creator and no money, focus is the whole game:

1. **Lead with demand, not "both equally."** You said both sides at once. In practice a maintenance
   marketplace lives or dies on having _homeowners and landlords with jobs to post_. Contractors
   will follow paid work. So ~**70% of effort → homeowners/landlords, 30% → contractors.** Don't try
   to split 50/50, you'll do neither well.
2. **Go deep in one city.** A marketplace needs density: enough jobs and enough trades in the _same
   postcode_ to actually match. National reach with 150 users is just noise. Pick your city, name it
   in your bio, and win it.
3. **The "150 lifetime-free founding members" offer is your single best hook.** Scarcity +
   generosity + "get in before everyone else." Build the whole 30 days around filling those 150
   seats.

## Content pillars (rotate these, never post randomly)

1. **Transformation / proof** _(IG-led, highest reach)_, before/after of real jobs, satisfying fix
   clips, "this took 2 hours." Even if you don't have your own jobs yet, partner with one or two
   friendly local tradespeople and feature _their_ work (free promo for them, content for you).
2. **The trust problem** _(both platforms)_, the thing your whole product exists to fix. "Why 1 in 3
   people dread hiring a tradesperson." Escrow, vetting, reviews, explain _how Mintenance removes
   the risk_ without sounding like an ad.
3. **Build in public** _(LinkedIn-led, your unfair advantage as a technical founder)_, "We're two
   founders building the maintenance marketplace the UK actually needs." Show the product, the beta,
   the wins and the stumbles. People back people. This is the cheapest, highest-trust content you
   have and almost nobody competes with you on it.
4. **Educational / service** _(both)_, "5 questions to ask before you pay a deposit," "what a fair
   quote for X looks like," landlord compliance reminders (gas safety, EICR). Useful = saved +
   shared = reach.
5. **The founding-member offer** _(both, your call-to-action pillar)_, the 150 free-for-life seats.
   Run it as a visible, shrinking countdown ("113 seats left"). Pin it. Repeat it.

Rough weekly mix across 4–5 posts: **2 transformation/proof · 1 build-in-public · 1 educational or
trust · 1 founding-member push** (the offer also rides along inside the other four).

## The founding-member campaign (the spine of the 30 days)

- **Offer:** First 150 users get Mintenance free for life. Frame it as a thank-you to the people who
  help shape the product, not a discount.
- **Mechanism:** A dead-simple landing page or even a Google/Typeform → collect name, email,
  postcode, and "homeowner or contractor." Postcode matters, it tells you where your density is
  forming.
- **Make scarcity visible:** Update the number publicly every few days. "47 founding homeowners in
  [city] so far." Shrinking numbers create urgency for free.
- **Two tracks, one campaign:** homeowners get "free for life, skip the stress of finding a
  tradesperson"; contractors get "free for life, first access to local jobs."

## Where the first users actually come from (content alone won't do it)

Posting into an account with few followers is shouting into an empty room. For the first ~150,
**distribution beats production**. Spend as much time placing content as making it:

- **Local Facebook groups & Nextdoor**, "[City] community", "[City] landlords", "Recommend a
  tradesperson [City]". These are _full_ of your exact homeowner. Be helpful first, don't spam;
  mention the founding offer when relevant.
- **Letting / estate agents**, landlords are your highest-value demand. A handful of small local
  agencies signing up brings recurring maintenance jobs. Warm DM + LinkedIn outreach.
- **Trade Facebook groups & r/DIYUK, r/HousingUK**, for the contractor seed and for homeowner
  pain-point listening.
- **Your own + Liam's networks**, the first 20 founding members should come from people you can DM
  directly. Don't be too proud to ask.
- **Comment, don't just post**, 15 min/day commenting usefully on local property and trades accounts
  will out-perform an extra post.

## What to measure (ignore follower count)

Track these weekly in a simple sheet:

- **Founding-member signups** (the only number that really matters, target ~37/week to hit 150 in 30
  days; realistically front-load expectations low and ramp)
- **Signups by postcode** (is density forming anywhere?)
- **Saves + shares** on IG (reach signal, far more important than likes)
- **Profile visits → link clicks** (is content driving action?)
- **DMs / comments started** (early demand signal)

If a post type drives signups, do more of it. If it only gets likes, it's entertainment, not
marketing.

## Sustainable weekly rhythm (solo-founder friendly)

You're the only creator, so batch, don't create daily from scratch:

- **Sunday (60–90 min):** batch-shoot/write the week's 4–5 posts. Repurpose one idea across both
  platforms (an IG before/after becomes a LinkedIn "here's why trust is broken in this industry"
  post).
- **Daily (15–20 min):** post the scheduled one, then comment in 3–5 local groups / accounts.
- **Friday (15 min):** update the founding-member counter publicly + log the week's numbers.

One genuine job story, filmed once, can become: an IG reel, an IG carousel, a LinkedIn post, and a
"X seats left" CTA. Make once, cut four ways.
